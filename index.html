How it works

Simple, dignified, and always open

Our pantry is a small stocked cabinet on a front lawn — like a little free library, but for pets. Anyone can stop by anytime.

🤲

Take what you need

Grab food, treats, litter, or supplies for your pet. No forms, no proof, no questions. If your pet needs it, it's yours.

🎁

Leave what you can

Have a little extra? Drop off unopened pet food or supplies so the next neighbor finds a full shelf. Every bit helps.

💛

It takes a village

The pantry runs entirely on neighbors looking out for one another and the animals they love. You make it possible.

Give

Help keep the shelves full

Demand is highest for the basics. Unopened, in-date items are always welcome — drop them in the pantry anytime, or reach out for a larger donation.

  • 🐕 Dry dog food
  • 🐈 Dry cat food
  • 🥫 Wet food (cans/pouches)
  • 🧴 Cat litter
  • 🦴 Treats & chews
  • 🧼 Flea & tick care
  • 🥣 Bowls & leashes
  • 🧸 Toys & bedding

Three ways to give

1. Drop it off. Leave unopened supplies in the pantry anytime — see Visit for the location.

2. Give funds. A few dollars buys food during shortages and helps us grow.

3. Spread the word. Tell a neighbor who could use it, or who'd love to give.

Donate or organize a drop-off

Money donation link coming soon. For now, email us and we'll set it up.

Visit

Find the pantry

You'll find us on the 200 block of Highland Avenue in East Pittsburgh, PA 15112 — a small pantry cabinet out front. Use the map for the general block; you'll spot the 🐾 sign when you're close.

Open 24/7. If the shelf is bare or you need something specific, just email us — we're glad to help.

What's next · Vet Van Fleet

From a pantry to a movement

Free Pet Pantry is the first step. We're building toward Vet Van Fleet — a nonprofit initiative bringing free veterinary care to pets everywhere, wherever they are. Food keeps pets fed; healthcare keeps them well. We believe every pet deserves both.
